Python Project: HDI & GDP Analysis

Overview

This Python project showcases the relationship between human development and economic growth.

Jupyter Notebook

Key Insights

  • GDP is a driver, but not the sole determinant of HDI. While GDP and HDI are positively correlated, outliers in regions like South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa demonstrate that social policies, governance, and resource efficiency also play critical roles.
  • Regions like SSA face systemic challenges with low GDP and low HDI clustering, while regions like EAP and ECA show greater variance, including high-GDP and high-HDI outliers.
  • Countries with moderate GDP but better HDI outcomes (e.g., Bhutan, Cabo Verde) provide models of effective social investment, highlighting the importance of governance and resource allocation.

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Invest in Social Development Beyond Economic Growth:
    While GDP is positively correlated with HDI, countries with similar GDP levels often have widely varying HDI outcomes.
    • Policy Focus: Prioritize investments in education, healthcare, and social welfare to enhance quality of life, even in low- or middle-income economies.
    • Learn from success stories like Bhutan (South Asia) and Cabo Verde (Sub-Saharan Africa), where efficient resource allocation has led to relatively high HDI despite modest GDP.
  2. Strengthen Governance and Policy Implementation:
    Countries with moderate GDP but better HDI outcomes demonstrate the importance of strong governance and targeted social policies.
    • Policy Focus: Encourage transparent governance systems that prioritize human development indicators alongside economic performance.
    • Implement data-driven approaches to track and address development gaps.
